My Future Mapping Projects & Goals

Below are some of the mapping projects I plan on working on next:

Attending State of the Map 2026

I am looking forward to attending the 2026 State of the Map conference in Paris. I am hoping to learn more about OpenStreetMap contributions, particularly how HOT data is used once it has been published. I'm also looking forward to getting to know different data processing methods, mapping techniques, and tech developments in the world of GIS.

Making a 3D "Spiky Map"

I was inspired by the 2005 article published by The Atlantic Monthly called "The World is Spiky". The map in particular stuck with me, because it showed me a whole new way of visualizing data that I had never seen before. Inspired by this article, I am planning to learn how to create 3D "spike" maps to visualize data like population density, air/noise pollution, etc. I think it's a very fun and striking way of representing data, and I'd love to give it a try.

Modeling (and Mapping) Climate Patterns

I have made maps showing live precipitation, cloud cover, and temperature before, using OpenWeatherMap's API. However, I would like to go a step further and make a map showing wind/precipitation/temperature patterns over time. Meteorology is something that has interested me for a very long time, and making a basic model showing how weather behaves would be a fun and insightful challenge.
